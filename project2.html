Smart Station – Interactive Trolley Return System

Role: Lead UX/UI Designer
Duration: 6 Weeks
Key Achievement: Increased trolley returns by 50% in pilot study.
View the Full Report
quiz

The Problem

Supermarket trolleys are often left scattered in parking lots, creating hazards and requiring manual collection. The existing return process lacks incentives and clear instructions, leading to low compliance from shoppers.

attribution

David, Busy Shopper

"A parent who wants to get in and out of the supermarket as quickly as possible."

Needs: Clear instructions, Quick process

Frustrations: Ambiguous signage, Processes that take too much time

My Role & The Team

On this project, I was responsible for the following key areas:

  • User Journey Mapping
  • Physical Computing
  • Interaction Prototyping

Research & Discovery

Observed shopper behavior in 3 different supermarket parking lots. Found that only 2 out of 10 shoppers returned their trolleys to the designated bay if it was more than 50 feet away.

"I'd return it if I knew where it went and if it wasn't so far away. I just want to get home."

The Journey: From Ideation to Solution

lightbulbIdeation & Wireframing

Focused on creating a "moment of delight" in a mundane task. Sketched a "Wheel of Prizes" concept to act as a powerful incentive for users to engage with the system.

task_altUsability Testing

Tested a physical prototype with a mock-up tablet interface. Users were excited by the game but confused about how to scan their receipt to begin.

Key Finding:

The first step of the process was a major friction point. The "Scan Here" instruction wasn't visible enough."

model_trainingKey Iteration

The tablet UI was redesigned to have a large, animated "Start Here: Scan Your Receipt" button as the very first screen.

The Change:

This change eliminated all confusion during the initial step, reducing the average transaction time from 45 seconds to 15 seconds.

arrow_drop_down
design_services

The Solution: High-Fidelity Designs

We designed “Smart Station,” an interactive system that gamifies the trolley return process. It includes a public display and a tablet interface. By offering a chance to win a prize, we motivate users, while the streamlined interface makes the process quick and easy.

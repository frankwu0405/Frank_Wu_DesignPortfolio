Interactive Learning Tools for Remote Students

Role: Lead UX/UI Designer
Duration: 6 Weeks
Key Achievement: Increased student engagement by 40% in pilot testing.
View the Full Report
quiz

The Problem

Students in remote and rural areas often lack access to the same quality of STEM educational resources as their urban counterparts. This educational gap is compounded by a lack of engaging, interactive digital tools that can function in low-connectivity environments.

attribution

Maria, 8th Grade Student

"I am curious about science but find it difficult to stay focused during my online lessons at home."

Needs: Interactive content

Goals: Visual explanations

Motivations: Can learn at home

Frustrations: Lack of engaging study materials

My Role & The Team

On this project, I was responsible for the following key areas:

  • User Research
  • UI Design
  • Wireframing & Prototyping
  • Usability Testing

I collaborated with a team of 3 Developers, 1 Project Manager.

Research & Discovery

Conducted interviews with 15 remote students. Found that 80% felt their science materials were 'uninteresting' and 'hard to visualize'.

"I wish I could actually see how the water cycle works instead of just reading about it."

arrow_drop_down
moving

The Journey: From Ideation to Solution

lightbulbIdeation & Wireframing

Sketched multiple concepts for gamified modules, focusing on turning abstract scientific concepts into visual puzzles. Wireframes prioritized a simple, visual hierarchy for easy navigation.

task_altUsability Testing

Ran usability tests on a clickable prototype with 5 students. They completed all tasks, but navigation anticipated but struggled to find the "Progress" page.

Key Finding:

"Users were highly engaged with the game but found the linear navigation obvious way to return their overall progress in the "My Progress" tab."

model_trainingKey Iteration

Based on testing, the navigation was redesigned to include a persistent bottom bar for easy access to a "Progress" tab.

The Change:

A prominent progress bar and dedicated progress page was added, which increased user satisfaction scores by 25%.

The Solution: High-Fidelity Designs

We developed a mobile-first, gamified learning application that provides interactive STEM modules, which work offline. By transforming complex topics into engaging games, we significantly increased student engagement and knowledge retention.

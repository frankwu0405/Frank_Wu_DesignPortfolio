The Problem

The shift to online fashion shopping has amplified an environmental crisis. About 30% all online fashion purchases are returned, creating millions of tons of waste annually due to customer dissatisfaction. This project aims to address the experiential gap between online and physical retail to reduce waste by increasing purchase satisfaction.

The Journey: From Ideation to Solution

lightbulbIdeation & Wireframing

Brainstormed features to address the 'fit' problem, leading to the core concept of an AI-driven app, powered by a user's customizable virtual self.

task_altUsability Testing

Through heuristic evaluation and Think-Aloud protocol, the insights sparked a few area of improvement:

#1 Lack of System Status

I had a hard time figuring out how many items I'm trying at the moment and if better the total estimated price for all of them.

#2 Unclear Icons & Buttons

I think you might need to have a bit more explanation on that part. Either next to the arrow or directly available from the fitting room.

#3 Error Prevention & Intuitive Shortcuts

Once I tried the item on in the fitting room page, I could no longer find where I could go and put the item in the bag.

model_trainingKey Iteration

Following the testing, the iteration focused on improving status visibility, function availability, and color contrast to maximise interaction experience.

The Solution: High-Fidelity Designs

Our final design, One Shop, is a mobile-centered fashion e-commerce application that addresses the environmental waste from high product return rates by reducing the experiential gap between online and physical shopping. Utilizing an AI-powered fitting room visualizer, shoppers can have a better grasp on product compatibility and further boost purchase confidence before they purchase.
